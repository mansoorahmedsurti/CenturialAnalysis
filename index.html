5th Century

The 5th century marked the fall of empires and the rise of new civilizations shaping the medieval world.

  • Major Events
    1. Fall of the Western Roman Empire (476 CE)
    2. Anglo-Saxon settlement of Britain begins
  • Major Empires
    1. Eastern Roman (Byzantine) Empire
    2. Hunnic Empire (until 453 CE)
  • Major Conflicts
    1. Sack of Rome by Visigoths (410 CE)
    2. Battle of Chalons (451 CE)
  • Population Trends
    1. Decline in Western urban populations
    2. Mass displacement due to migrations.
  • Key Leaders
    1. Attila (Hun)
    2. Clovis I (Frank)
    3. Odoacer (Germanic/Herule)
  • Key Intellectuals
    1. Saint Augustine (Theology/Philosophy, The City of God)
    2. Mesrop Mashtots (Linguistics, Creation of Armenian alphabet)
  • Major Religions
    1. Christianity (Consolidation and spread, Council of Chalcedon)
  • Currency
    1. Byzantine Gold Solidus (Dominant stable currency).
