4th Century CE Overview

4th Century CE

The 4th century CE saw the division of the Roman Empire and the official adoption of Christianity as a state religion.

  • Major Events
    1. Edict of Milan (313 CE)
    2. Division of Roman Empire (395 CE)
  • Major Empires
    1. Eastern Roman Empire (Byzantine)
    2. Western Roman Empire
  • Major Conflicts
    1. Gothic Wars
    2. Internal power struggles in Rome
  • Population Trends
    1. Urban decline in Western provinces, Eastern cities thrive
  • Key Leaders
    1. Constantine the Great
    2. Theodosius I
  • Key Intellectuals
    1. St. Augustine (Theology)
  • Major Religions
    1. Christianity (official religion), Roman Religion, Paganism
  • Currency
    1. Solidus (gold coin), Debased Denarius
